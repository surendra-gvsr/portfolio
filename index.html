Manufacturing Quality Control Analysis

Overview

Analyzed manufacturing defect patterns across shifts to improve product quality and reduce waste. Using 30 production periods of automated data, identified key factors affecting defect rates and delivered actionable recommendations for quality improvement.

Key Findings

Temperature Impact: Strong correlation (0.93) between temperature variability and defect rates

Shift Analysis: Morning shift showed 2.2x higher defect rate (37.36 vs 16.92 per 1000 units)

Production Speed: Significant interaction between production rate and morning shift quality

Solution & Impact

Developed a comprehensive quality improvement strategy:

• Implemented differential production rates between shifts

• Established real-time temperature monitoring system

• Created targeted training programs for morning shift

Projected outcomes include reduced quality variations, lower defect rates, and decreased waste costs.

Tools Used

R, EDA, Descriptive Statistics, Linear and Multiple Regression

Super Bowl Advertising Impact Analysis

Overview

Analyzed Super Bowl advertising effectiveness for three car brands (Beetle, Camaro, CR-Z) by examining 15 weeks of social media engagement data. Investigated relationships between ad spend and social media metrics to optimize future marketing investments.

Key Findings

Pre vs Post Super Bowl correlation shift (0.975 to 0.0513) between ad spend and engagement

Beetle: Highest ROI with 46.52% increase in positive mentions

Camaro: Maintained highest absolute engagement (93,613 mentions)

Content quality proved more important than pure ad spend

Solution & Impact

Developed targeted recommendations for each brand:

• Created brand-specific engagement frameworks

• Established real-time monitoring systems

• Designed integrated marketing strategies beyond Super Bowl

Results showed that effective Super Bowl advertising requires balanced focus on brand positioning and content quality, not just spending levels.

Tools Used

R, EDA, Univariate and Bi-variate Statistics

PopRunner Digital Marketing Analysis

Overview

Evaluated effectiveness of email campaigns versus pop-up advertisements in driving sales and customer engagement. Analyzed customer demographics, engagement metrics, and purchase behavior to optimize digital marketing strategy.

Key Findings

Email marketing showed superior performance ($240.83 avg. purchase vs $126.12 for non-engaged)

Pop-up ads reached more customers (50% vs 7.93% email) but led to lower sales

Customer base predominantly female (76.43%) with varied loyalty levels

Higher loyalty levels correlated with increased age and spending

Solution & Impact

Developed targeted strategy focusing on:

• Email marketing expansion with personalized content

• Pop-up redesign based on timing and targeting

• Loyalty-level specific campaign development

• Enhanced tracking and metrics collection

Results projected to improve customer engagement and sales through optimized channel usage.

Tools Used

SQL, R


Healthcare Cost Prediction Analysis

Overview

Analyzed medical cost data from 1,338 individuals to identify key factors influencing healthcare expenses. Developed predictive models to understand the relationship between demographic factors, health metrics, and medical costs.

Key Findings

Smoking status is the primary cost driver ($23,616 average increase)

Age increases medical costs by $258 per year

Healthy BMI reduces costs by $3,466 on average

Model explains 73.4% of cost variation

Gender split nearly equal (50.52% male, 49.48% female)

Solution & Impact

Developed targeted recommendations:

• Created smoking cessation program framework

• Designed BMI management initiatives

• Established age-specific health programs

• Implemented comprehensive wellness tracking

Results projected to significantly reduce healthcare costs through preventive care and lifestyle modifications.

Tools Used

R, Statistical Analysis Tools, Predictive Modeling

Boston Housing Price Analysis

Overview

Analyzed housing price data from 506 Boston neighborhoods to identify key price determinants and develop an optimal prediction model. Investigated relationships between housing values and various neighborhood characteristics.

Key Findings

Room count and lower status population percentage are strongest predictors (48.4% and 54.4% variance explained)

Charles River proximity increases value by average $2,690

Optimized model explains 79.8% of price variation

Found significant interaction between room count and neighborhood status

Solution & Impact

Developed comprehensive pricing framework:

• Created transformed variable model for improved accuracy

• Established interaction-based prediction system

• Designed neighborhood-specific valuation guidelines

• Built scalable prediction framework

Results enable more accurate property valuations considering both physical and demographic factors.

Tools Used

R, Statistical Modeling, Regression Analysis Tools

Customer Churn Prediction Analysis

Overview

Developed and compared machine learning models to predict customer churn using data from 1,000 customers. Analyzed key factors influencing customer retention and identified high-risk customers for targeted intervention.

Key Findings

Random Forest model achieved 81.6% accuracy (vs Decision Tree's 79.3%)

Identified 26 high-risk customers with >70% churn probability

Agreement period emerged as the strongest predictor

Current churn rate at 25.9% of customer base

Model comparison revealed superior performance of Random Forest

Solution & Impact

Implemented comprehensive retention strategy:

• Developed automated risk scoring system

• Created tiered intervention framework

• Established model performance monitoring

• Designed proactive retention programs

Results enable targeted retention efforts for high-risk customers.

Tools Used

R, Machine Learning (Random Forest, Decision Trees), Modal Building
